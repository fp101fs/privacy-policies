Privacy Policy for AI AutoComment (OpenRouter Edition)

Effective Date: February 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how AI AutoComment (OpenRouter Edition) ("the Extension") handles information. We are committed to protecting your privacy.

1. Information We Do Not Collect
We (the developer) do not collect, store, transmit, or access any personal information from users. This includes:
- No names, email addresses, IP addresses, browsing history, or any personally identifiable information (PII).
- No usage analytics, telemetry, crash reports, or tracking of any kind.
- No data is sent to the developer or any servers controlled by the developer.

2. What the Extension Does With Your Data
The Extension is designed to generate AI-powered comment drafts based on page content, using your own OpenRouter API key.

- Your OpenRouter API key: You enter it manually in the Extension popup/side panel. It is stored **only locally** on your device using Chrome's storage API (chrome.storage.local). We never see, store, or transmit your API key.
- Page content: When you explicitly click "Scan Page Content" and then "Draft Comment", the Extension temporarily reads relevant visible text from the current webpage (using activeTab and scripting permissions) and sends **only that content** (along with your API key) directly to OpenRouter's API[](https://openrouter.ai) to generate the comment draft. This is a user-initiated request — nothing is sent automatically or in the background.
- OpenRouter handles the AI processing according to their own privacy policy: https://openrouter.ai/privacy (we have no control over or access to OpenRouter's data practices).

3. No Other Data Sharing
We do not share, sell, or disclose any information to third parties (except the direct, user-triggered transmission to OpenRouter described above). There are no ads, trackers, or analytics in the Extension.

4. Changes to This Policy
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted here with an updated effective date. Continued use of the Extension after changes constitutes acceptance.
